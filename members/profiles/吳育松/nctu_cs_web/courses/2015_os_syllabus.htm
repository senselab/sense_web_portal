Overview

This is the graduate-level operating systems course. You should be familiar with all the basic operating systems concepts, as taught in the undergraduate operating systems course, before taking this course.

 

Operating systems is a vast area. We make no attempt to cover all the exciting topics in this course. For each selected topic, we will begin with a quick overview of the background knowledge (again, we assume you are already very familiar with it), and then we will dive into some sub-topics in great detail. Specifically, we have selected a few seminal papers on each sub-topic. You are required to read the papers and participate in the discussions of the papers. The exam questions will be based on materials from the papers.

 

To give you the opportunity to study a particular area of OS in greater detail, you are also required to conduct a project in this course. The project involves selecting an open problem, reading the related work, designing, implementing, and evaluating a solution, and presenting your results.
